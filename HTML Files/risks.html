Money Traps & Scam Awareness

Slow down when money sounds too easy

Search common traps and learn the warning signs before you trust anyone with your money.

Gambling

Gambling promises excitement but usually has odds designed against the player. It can become addictive and destroy savings.

Fake Trading Gurus

Some people sell dreams using rented cars, edited screenshots, and fake profit claims. Real skill does not guarantee easy income.

Ponzi Schemes

Ponzi schemes use new investor money to pay old investors. They collapse when new money stops coming in.

MLM Scams

MLM traps often focus more on recruiting people than selling useful products. Most participants may earn little or lose money.

Fake Loan Apps

Fake loan apps may steal contacts, charge illegal fees, and harass borrowers. Use only trusted regulated lenders.

Crypto Rug Pulls

A rug pull happens when project creators disappear with investor funds after hyping a token or project.

Telegram Signal Scams

Signal groups may show selective wins, hide losses, and push paid memberships or risky trades.

Quick Rich Schemes

Guaranteed high returns with no risk are a major warning sign. Real wealth usually needs time, skill, and discipline.

Over Leverage

Leverage increases buying power but also increases losses. A small move can wipe out large capital.

Revenge Trading

Revenge trading means taking impulsive trades after a loss to recover quickly. It usually makes losses worse.
