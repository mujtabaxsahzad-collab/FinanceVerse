Investment Guide

Understand investment choices before using them

Learn what each investment means, where it may help, and what risk beginners should respect.

IPO

An IPO is when a company sells shares to the public for the first time. It can be exciting, but listing price can move up or down quickly.

Stocks

Stocks are small ownership units in a company. Their value changes based on business performance, market mood, and future expectations.

Mutual Funds

Mutual funds collect money from many investors and invest it through a professional fund manager across stocks, bonds, or other assets.

SIP

A SIP lets you invest a fixed amount regularly, usually monthly. It helps build discipline and reduces the pressure of timing the market.

ETF

An ETF is a fund that trades like a stock. Many ETFs track an index, sector, commodity, or market theme.

Bonds

Bonds are loans given to governments or companies. Investors usually receive interest and get principal back at maturity, subject to risk.

Fixed Deposit

A fixed deposit locks money for a chosen period at a fixed interest rate. It is simple, but returns may be lower than inflation at times.

Gold

Gold is often used as a hedge during uncertainty. It may protect value, but it does not create business income like companies do.

Real Estate

Real estate includes land, homes, and commercial property. It can provide rent and appreciation, but needs large capital and maintenance.

Beginner comparisons

IPO vs Stocks

IPO investing happens at first public sale. Stock investing can happen anytime after listing. IPOs may have hype; listed stocks offer more price history.

Stocks vs Mutual Funds

Stocks require choosing companies yourself. Mutual funds provide diversification and professional management, but include expense ratios.

SIP vs FD

SIP returns are market-linked and uncertain. FD returns are fixed and predictable, but usually lower over long periods.

Gold vs Real Estate

Gold is easier to buy in small amounts. Real estate needs more money, paperwork, and maintenance, but can generate rent.
