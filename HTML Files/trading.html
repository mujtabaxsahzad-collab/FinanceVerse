Trading Guide

Trading is risk management first

Learn trading styles and the safety rules beginners must understand before risking money.

Scalping

Scalping means taking very quick trades for small price moves. It needs speed, discipline, and strict costs control.

Intraday

Intraday traders open and close trades on the same day. They avoid overnight risk but face high decision pressure.

Swing Trading

Swing trading holds positions for days or weeks to capture short-term trends. It needs patience and planned exits.

Positional Trading

Positional trading holds for weeks or months based on broader trends. It is slower than intraday but still needs risk control.

Forex Trading

Forex trading involves currency pairs. Leverage can be high, so losses can become large very quickly.

Commodity Trading

Commodity trading includes assets like crude oil, gold, and agricultural goods. Prices can react strongly to global events.

Futures Trading

Futures are contracts to buy or sell later at a set price. They use margin and can create large gains or losses.

Options Trading

Options give rights based on future price movement. They are complex and beginners should learn Greeks, expiry, and risk first.

Trading safety basics

Trading vs Investing

Trading focuses on shorter price movements. Investing focuses on long-term ownership and compounding.

Risk Management

Risk management means deciding how much you can lose before entering a trade. It protects your capital.

Stop Loss

A stop loss is a planned exit if the trade goes wrong. It should be set before emotion takes over.

Position Sizing

Position sizing controls trade quantity so one bad trade cannot damage your account badly.

Overtrading

Overtrading happens when you trade too often due to excitement, boredom, or revenge after a loss.
